Paprika Pork Shoulder Braise
Aaron Scott with Copilot
A dark, paprika-forward braise designed for a 35-oz pork shoulder, yielding tender, cohesive pieces without shredding.


35 oz. pork shoulder, cut into large chunks after brining 1 to 1 1/4 cups broth (beef or chicken)
2 large onions, sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced
2 tbsp. tomato paste 1 bay leaf
2 tbsp. sweet paprika 1 tsp. dried thyme or marjoram
½ tsp. hot paprika (optional) Salt (preferably smoked) and black pepper, to taste
½ cup red wine 2 tbsp. oil or fat for browning
½ to 3/4 cup white wine

1.Pat the buttermilk-brined pork dry and cut into large, palm-sized pieces. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a heavy pot
and brown the pork well on all sides, then remove and set aside.
2. Add the sliced onions to the pot and cook until softened and beginning to color. Stir in the tomato paste and cook 1-2 minutes until
darkened. Add garlic and paprika, stirring briefly to bloom the spices. Pour in the red and white wine, scraping up any browned bits.
3. Add enough broth to bring the total liquid to about 2 to 2 1/2 cups. Return the pork to the pot, add bay leaf and thyme, and bring to
a gentle simmer. Cover and braise in a 300°F oven for 2 to 2 1/2 hours, or until the pork is tender but still holds together cleanly
when cut. Rest the meat in the sauce for 15 minutes before serving.

Browning the tomato paste is essential for achieving a deep, brick-red sauce. Adjust the ratio of red to white wine based on
availability; paprika and tomato paste will maintain the dark color. A small splash of vinegar at the end can brighten the flavors. Serve
with noodles, potatoes, or crusty bread.
